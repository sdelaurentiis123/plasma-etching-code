What petch does — one page

The whole project in one screen: what it is, what's validated, against what, and what isn't.

petch is a developing 3-D feature-scale plasma-etch simulator: dimensional boundary distributions, conservative particle/radiosity transport, stateful surface mechanisms, charging, and level-set profile evolution. A common evidence-bearing engine now exists beside the older ViennaPS-shaped compatibility path. CPU and selected CUDA kernels run; an accuracy-matched end-to-end performance claim and feature-scale differentiability remain open.

Current evidence headlines

  1. The common engine closes its internal ledgers. Charged-particle lineage, neutral source/react/escape balance, target-material exchange, moving-surface charge remap, and profile motion are explicit and tested.
  2. The direct de Boer comparison falsified the old story. The historical 1/0.43/0.29/0.20 sequence is a calculated Clausing curve, not directly measured markers. Against 16 digitized Figure-9 points, the old common-engine adapter missed the twelve transfer points by 5.55 µm RMSE on the finest completed grid. Those points are now development-only.
  3. The reusable silicon path now runs end to end. Common-engine Belen chemistry, neutral radiosity, certified grazing-ion reflection, charge/energy lineage, and periodic moving-profile evolution run through one operator. On the exposed de Boer Figure-9 development data, reflection improves transfer RMSE from 3.54 to 2.56 µm, but an independent experiment is still required for validation.
  4. Charging/notching/twisting machinery exists, but the experimental milestone is open. Physical-time charging and stochastic ensemble/profile observables run through the common engine; the Nozawa held-out notch campaign and N≥30 twist campaign have not yet passed.

Honest limits

No independent feature-profile validation has passed yet. The direct de Boer Figure-9 pixels are now exposed development data; the common Belen + reflection repair materially improves the miss but does not close it. The historical Knudsen/Clausing curve is retained for replay only, not scored as experiment. DDA is quarantined in the mirror-wall regime. Charging/notching has tested mechanism infrastructure, but the Nozawa held-out campaign and absolute notch-depth validation remain open. Twisting remains statistical machinery awaiting its N≥30 campaign. Every number above has a reproducer script and a pass/fail gate in the repo; RECONCILIATION.md contains the full audit trail and retractions.
