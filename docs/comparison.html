Scorecard — petch vs measured data vs other solvers

Every claim is a gate against published or measured data, with a reproducer script. Where petch is behind, it says so.

Two questions decide whether a feature-scale etcher is worth using: does it match real wafers (not just another simulator), and can it do things the standard tool can't? This page puts petch beside ViennaPS (the open standard everyone benchmarks) and the Kushner-group Monte-Carlo codes (HPEM / MCFPM, the research reference for charging), against the measured data all three answer to.

Where petch is behind — stated plainly

ViennaPS is mature and broadly validated across many chemistries and process conditions; petch's experimental validation is one wafer dataset, one chemistry (de Boer cryo SF6/O2) so far. petch's charging notch depth is not yet calibrated in absolute µm (~3–5× shallow of Hwang–Giapis; the shape and the on/off signature are what pass). The deterministic DDA transport is quarantined in the mirror-wall regime. Charging is insulating floors only, and the deep-AR electron supply (RF-phase trajectories) is an open item. Kushner's codes couple the reactor to the feature; petch takes incident fluxes as input (like ViennaPS). Nothing here is hidden — see Experiments and the repo's RECONCILIATION.md (retractions included).

2. The killer plots

#1 — Real wafer, held-out prediction

The accuracy claim that isn't circular: calibrate one transport knob on the de Boer knee (AR 10/20), then predict the AR 40 point. It lands on the measured wafer. Ballistic codes (ViennaPS included) roll off too fast in the deep tail.

#2 — Charging vs the published gate (ViennaPS has no charging at all)

petch carries a 2-D Hwang–Giapis charging solver built directly on the published method — ballistic ion/electron tracing through the in-feature field, per-segment current balance, Laplace in the vacuum.

#3 — Charging-driven notching (nobody in petch's class does this)

The charging solver wired into an overetch: multi-material etch-stop (poly on buried oxide), and during overetch the deflected ion flux digs a notch at the poly/oxide junction — the measured Hwang–Giapis / Fujiwara phenomenon.

#4 — Deterministic transport vs a measured ViennaPS reference

The like-for-like numerics cross-check: petch's deterministic neutral transport against a measured static ViennaPS ARDE curve, in the same geometry.

Bottom line

Against measured data, petch predicts a real wafer's deep ARDE tail it was never fit to, and reproduces the Hwang–Giapis charging curve with zero tuned constants. Against ViennaPS, it matches the transport numerics and runs ~8–15× faster, while adding two whole physics channels ViennaPS lacks — charging and charging-driven notching. Against the Kushner-group codes, it trades reactor coupling and decades of validation breadth for being open, GPU-resident, and differentiable. The honest gaps — absolute notch calibration, one dataset, DDA's mirror-wall regime — are on the table, not under it.
