Scorecard — petch vs measured data vs other solvers

Every claim is a gate against published or measured data, with a reproducer script. Where petch is behind, it says so.

Two questions decide whether a feature-scale etcher is worth using: does it match real wafers (not just another simulator), and can it do things the standard tool can't? This page puts petch beside ViennaPS (the open standard everyone benchmarks) and the Kushner-group Monte-Carlo codes (HPEM / MCFPM, the research reference for charging), against the measured data all three answer to.

Where petch is behind — stated plainly

ViennaPS is mature and broadly validated across many chemistries and process conditions; petch has not yet passed an independent feature-profile validation. The direct de Boer pixels are development data, and the old Clausing-curve score is retired. petch's charging notch depth is not yet calibrated in absolute µm. The deterministic DDA transport is quarantined in the mirror-wall regime. Kushner's codes couple the reactor to the feature; petch takes incident fluxes as input (like ViennaPS). Nothing here is hidden — see Experiments and the repo's RECONCILIATION.md (retractions included).

2. The killer plots

#1 — Retired ARDE claim and current development result

The old chart replayed a calculated Blauw/Clausing curve and cannot support a wafer-validation claim. The direct Figure-9 audit is now authoritative: the common Belen mechanism plus certified grazing-ion reflection improves the twelve transfer-point RMSE from 3.54 to 2.56 µm, but the exposed data remain development-only and the miss remains scientifically meaningful.

#2 — Charging vs the published gate (ViennaPS has no charging at all)

petch carries a 2-D Hwang–Giapis charging solver built directly on the published method — ballistic ion/electron tracing through the in-feature field, per-segment current balance, Laplace in the vacuum.

#3 — Charging-driven notching (nobody in petch's class does this)

The charging solver wired into an overetch: multi-material etch-stop (poly on buried oxide), and during overetch the deflected ion flux digs a notch at the poly/oxide junction — the measured Hwang–Giapis / Fujiwara phenomenon.

#4 — Deterministic transport vs a measured ViennaPS reference

The like-for-like numerics cross-check: petch's deterministic neutral transport against a measured static ViennaPS ARDE curve, in the same geometry.

Bottom line

Against ViennaPS, petch has strong like-for-like transport cross-checks and an ~8–15× CPU-dependent speed result. It also carries charging and charging-driven morphology machinery ViennaPS lacks. Against experiment, however, feature-profile validation remains open: de Boer is now development data, Nozawa C4 has not run, and absolute notch calibration remains unresolved. Against the Kushner-group codes, petch trades reactor coupling and validation breadth for being open, GPU-resident, and differentiable.
