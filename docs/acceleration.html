Acceleration: The Full Speedup Catalog

Variance reduction, traversal, sparse data structures, and systems engineering — all expressions of one idea.

The unifying theme

In a Monte-Carlo etch simulator, the trench-floor roughness is variance. So every technique that reduces variance does three things at once: it makes the sim faster (fewer rays for the same noise floor), smoother (better-looking and physically cleaner), and differentiable (gradients survive). "Acceleration without loss of fidelity" is literally variance reduction.

1. Sampling & variance reduction

The standard error of a Monte-Carlo flux estimate is \(\propto\sigma/\sqrt{N}\). Every method below attacks either \(\sigma\) (the integrand variance) or the \(\sqrt{N}\) convergence rate.

TechniqueWhat it doesCost to fidelity
Quasi-Monte Carlo (Sobol)low-discrepancy directions fill sample space evenly; variance falls as \(\sim1/N\) instead of \(1/\sqrt N\) → ~10× fewer rays. Bonus: more coherent rays, helping RT-core throughput.none (exact)
Control variatecompute the direct, unobstructed flux in closed form (view factors) — exact — and Monte-Carlo only the small re-emission residual. Most of the signal becomes noise-free.none
Russian rouletteterminate low-weight re-emission paths probabilistically instead of a hard cap; replaces the biased \(n_{\rm re}=12\) truncation with an unbiased estimator.none (removes bias)
Next-event estimationat every bounce, deterministically connect to the source instead of waiting for a random walk to escape upward. Kills "did this path find the source" variance.none
Importance sampling + MISsample re-emission toward unshadowed / surface-bound directions; combine direct and re-emission estimators optimally (multiple importance sampling).none
Stratification / antitheticpartition the source plane and angular domain so samples can't clump; paired antithetic samples cancel low-order error.none
Adjoint / backward tracingtrace from the trench bottom you care about back to the source (reciprocity). When only the floor matters, far cheaper than forward.none
Bidirectionaltrace from source and from surface, connect in the middle — for deep features where forward paths rarely reach the bottom.none

2. The re-emission solve (neutrals)

TechniqueWhat it does
Deterministic discrete-ordinates (Sn) transport — ✓ shippedgather the neutral flux over a fixed cosine-weighted direction set marched through the level-set grid, re-emission iterated to convergence — noise-free, eliminates the \(1/\sqrt N\) floor for neutrals entirely. The petch deterministic option actually validated against ViennaPS ARDE (0.727 vs 0.73 @ AR 8.6); Warp kernel, ~0.1 s/eval on CUDA. (The Sn transport solver — not the "DDA" voxel traversal in §3.)
Radiosity linear solvesolve \((I-(1-s)K)^{-1}\Gamma_{\rm direct}\) for the neutral equilibrium — all bounces at once, zero noise, differentiable by adjoint. (See Flux & Transport §4.) Ported to petch (GMRES) but still needs calibration.
Hierarchical radiositycluster distant patches so the dense \(O(N^2)\) form-factor operator becomes \(O(N)\)–\(O(N\log N)\). (Hanrahan clustering.)
FMM / \(\mathcal{H}\)-matrix compressionfast-multipole or hierarchical-matrix compression of \(K\) for fast matrix-vector products and solves.
Anderson accelerationfewer iterations on the coverage↔sticking fixed point and on iterative radiosity solves.

3. Traversal & GPU execution

TechniqueWhat it does
DDA / sphere tracingmarch the level-set grid directly — no mesh, no BVH rebuild; SDF gives free big steps. (See Flux & Transport §5.)
BVH + RT cores\(O(\log M)\) ray-triangle traversal in hardware; peak throughput on coherent rays.
Wavefront / persistent-thread tracingqueue rays and process by stage to reduce warp divergence on bounce chains (vs. one megakernel).
Ray packetsbatch coherent rays for SIMD/warp-coherent traversal — fits the collimated ion beam.
Mixed precisionfp32 (OptiX-native) or fp16 ray math: more throughput, less memory.
Atomic-free scatteraccumulate flux to surface points by sort-then-segmented-reduce instead of contended atomics.
GPU-resident pipelinekeep \(\varphi\), mesh, and flux on-device across steps — no host round-trips. Often a 5–10× wall-clock win on its own.

4. Geometry & level set

TechniqueWhat it does
Narrow band / HRLEstore and update only near the surface — \(O(n^2)\) not \(O(n^3)\). The 3D enabler.
NanoVDB / sparse octreeGPU-friendly sparse voxels; pairs with DDA marching.
AMR / octree refinementfine cells only near the surface or high-curvature regions.
Lazy + local reinitializationreinit every \(k\) steps and only near the front — less numerical diffusion and less cost.
Semi-implicit advection / local substeppingrelax the CFL limit for bigger stable \(\Delta t\); sub-step only where \(F\) is large.

5. Chemistry & structure

TechniqueWhat it does
Stiff ODE integratorsRosenbrock / exponential integrators take big stable steps on the stiff coverage ODEs (vs. tiny explicit steps).
Symmetry / periodic unit cella dense hole array is translation-symmetric — simulate one cell with periodic BCs instead of a trillion holes. Often the single biggest real-world win.
Temporal coherence (warm start)flux fields barely change step-to-step on quasi-static deep sidewalls; warm-start each step from the last and re-sample only where the surface moved.
Spatial flux cachingcache the slowly-varying flux field and interpolate; recompute only where it changes (irradiance-caching analog).

7. Two benchmark axes — never conflate them

Measure speed and accuracy separately
  • Accuracy axis: Chamfer distance to ViennaPS, plus depth / sidewall-angle / ARDE. Moved by the model-form & numerical fixes (contributors 1–4, 5). Goal: match ViennaPS without rate_scale — now largely demonstrated (petch replicates ViennaPS's ballistic model; the deterministic Sn transport reproduces its ARDE). The live frontier has moved to matching the real wafer, not ViennaPS.
  • Speed axis: rays-to-fidelity (rays needed to hit a fixed noise floor) and rays/sec (raw throughput). Moved by the variance-reduction & traversal techniques above.

A speedup that quietly loses accuracy will hide if you only watch one axis. The convergence harness (grid refinement, ray refinement, Richardson extrapolation) watches both.
