The honest read

GPU ray tracing, radiosity, level sets, Monte-Carlo transport — all solved and shipping. None of these tools is differentiable, and none uses learned operators for the expensive physics. That negative space is the opportunity.

2. The field's lineage: Kushner and Graves

This project sits at the feature scale. The two scales that bracket it were built largely by two groups, and our simulator is the bridge between them.

Mark Kushner — the scale above us, and our methodological ancestor

  • HPEM (Hybrid Plasma Equipment Model) + GlobalKin (gas-phase chemistry kinetics) + PCMCM (Plasma Chemistry Monte Carlo Model) compute the reactor-scale plasma and deliver exactly the data we take as given: the species fluxes and the IED/IAD at the wafer. In the full vision, HPEM → PCMCM → our feature sim is the reactor-to-feature coupling.
  • MCFPM (Monte Carlo Feature Profile Model) is the canonical academic feature-scale etch profile code — pseudo-particles launched with energies/angles sampled from HPEM, ballistic transport, surface reaction probabilities. It is the direct ancestor of our forward Monte-Carlo transport. The key contrast: MCFPM is voxel/cell-based, whereas we (like ViennaPS) use a level set. Same physics, different surface representation.

David Graves — the scale below us, and the reaction model's foundation

  • Pioneered molecular-dynamics simulation of plasma-surface interactions: ion bombardment of Si in F/Cl chemistries, computing from first principles the very quantities our chemistry layer uses phenomenologically — etch/sputter yields \(Y(E,\theta)\), energy thresholds, the angular structure, sticking, and surface damage.
  • Classified the ion-enhanced etching mechanisms we lump into one rate: enhanced spontaneous etching, chemically enhanced physical sputtering, chemical sputtering — the microscopic content of the Coburn-Winters synergy.
  • Recent work uses Deep Potential MD (DeepMD) — machine-learned interatomic potentials — for Si-Cl-Ar etching, and MD of atomic layer etching. This is, at the atomistic scale, exactly the learned-operator idea on our Phase-2 page: replace expensive physics with a trained surrogate. It is the strongest signal that the foundation-model convergence is the right bet.
How they relate, in one sentence

Kushner gives us our inputs (plasma → fluxes/IED/IAD) and our method's ancestor (MCFPM); Graves gives us our reaction model (MD-derived yields and mechanisms) and a preview of the ML future (DeepMD). Our contribution is the feature-scale layer between them, made GPU-fast, differentiable, and operator-accelerated — which neither classic code is.

The RT-core caveat (keep it honest)

Hardware ray tracers hit their headline throughput (~10 GRays/s) only on coherent ray bundles. Diffuse neutral re-emission is incoherent, where RT cores underperform peak. This (a) tempers any "blazing on RT cores" claim and (b) is an argument for QMC and DDA, which are more coherent / coherence-agnostic. Do not pitch "faster ray tracer" — pitch "fewer rays + differentiable + learned operators."

Landed since (Phase 0/1 deliverables)

A deterministic discrete-ordinates (Sn) neutral transport has been absorbed from the sibling plasma_sim solver and reimplemented as a Warp kernel — it reproduces ViennaPS's ARDE (0.727 vs 0.73 @ AR 8.6) noise-free (ViennaPS Validation §7). Knudsen-tail and GMRES-radiosity neutral options are also ported, pending calibration. The Phase-1 head-to-head ran: petch is ~8–15× faster than ViennaPS-GPU (CPU-dependent — see Performance), matching on holes and ~0.06–0.1 gentler on trenches. (This Sn transport is distinct from the "DDA" voxel traversal named in Phase 0.)

5. Benchmark methodology

  • Accuracy: Chamfer distance to ViennaPS surface, plus depth, sidewall angle, ARDE slope. Target: match ViennaPS without rate_scale — now largely met (holes match; trenches are a converged ~0.06–0.1 gentler, a real source-discretization difference, not a failure — see ViennaPS Validation). The live accuracy target has moved to the real wafer.
  • Convergence: grid refinement \(\Delta x\to0\), ray refinement \(N\to\infty\) via Richardson extrapolation — separates discretization error from sampling noise.
  • Speed: rays-to-fidelity (rays to a fixed noise floor) and rays/sec. (See Acceleration §7.)

6. The reference code, mapped

The 2D proof-of-concept matches ViennaPS in shape and ARDE trend parameter-free, needing only a global rate_scale — an absolute-units constant (the analog of ViennaPS's unitConversion, not a fudge factor; its value tracks the flux-normalization convention — see Physics Grounding) — for absolute depth. The six contributors below are now second-order: petch already replicates ViennaPS's ballistic model (matching on holes, ~0.06–0.1 gentler on trenches), so the first-order residual is the structural ballistic-vs-wafer gap (gas conductance / charging), not these model-form refinements.
